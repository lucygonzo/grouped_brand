# The Grouped Brand Manifesto

## Make the screen disappear.

Every decision in this system serves one goal: make the digital experience feel physical. Tactile. Like an extension of your hands and ears. Music is not flat. It moves in cycles, in loops, in waves. The design should move the same way.


## Three Principles

### Cyclical
Music lives in loops. Write, record, release, connect, repeat. Every animation should feel like it's returning to something, building on the last cycle. Rounded corners, circular motifs, spring easings that overshoot and settle. Nothing stops. Everything flows into the next beat.

### Tactile
Glass has weight. Cards tilt when you reach for them. Stickers resist then follow your hand. Grain gives every surface a texture you can feel through the screen. The experience should remind you of handling vinyl, flipping through a crate, holding something real.

### Vibing
Timing is feel. Fast UI feels anxious. Slow UI feels confident. Transitions run 500ms or longer because we're not in a hurry. We're vibing. The cursor glow drifts. Cards lift and settle with weight. Everything breathes at a pace that matches sitting in a listening room, not clicking through a dashboard.


## The Feeling

This should feel like an extension of the artist, not a tool they log into.

When an artist opens Grouped, the screen should dissolve. No chrome, no clutter, no "app" feeling. Just them, their music, and a warm room full of fans. The physical metaphors in this system — glass, grain, warmth, tilt, weight — exist to bridge the gap between pixels and the real relationship happening underneath. Fans should feel the same thing: not a platform, but a place.


## The Room

Grouped is the room. Artists are the reason people walked in.

Every design decision should ask: does this elevate the artist's content, or compete with it? The deep blue recedes. The grain adds texture without noise. Glass surfaces frame without containing. When it works, artists feel like the platform was built around them. That is the goal.

Artist content should always be the brightest, warmest, most vivid thing on screen. The album art scales toward you when you hover, like picking a record up off the shelf. The card tilts in your hand. The shadow deepens underneath. These are the micro-moments that make a screen feel like a listening room and not a dashboard.


## The Temperature

Three temperature layers define the visual identity:

**Deep blue surfaces create the room.** Every dark surface carries a cool blue undertone — never neutral grey. It's the same reason jewelers display gold on dark blue velvet, not black fabric.

**Warm bronze marks the brand.** Headlines, logos, section labels, CTAs, and the concentric arc motif. Bronze is earned — it appears at moments of importance, not everywhere.

**Cool focus blue handles everything interactive.** Input highlights, accent stickers, active indicators, badges. It pops against deep blue without competing with the warmth of bronze.


## The Shapes

Every shape and interaction borrows from music.

**Light elements** — Waveforms, flowing lines, frequency curves. Thin, luminous, alive. Progress bars that ripple. Loading states that pulse. Hover trails that follow the cursor like sound moving through a room.

**Solid elements** — Volume dials, fader channels, patch badges. Heavy, grounded, tactile. Clean geometric shapes from studio hardware — circles that rotate, rectangles that slide. The shape itself communicates the function.


## Where Grouped Shows Up

The brand lives across every surface — digital and physical:

- **Product (web app)** — The primary experience. Dark mode default, warm cream text, bronze accents earned.
- **Marketing site** — Dark mode only. The manifesto made visual. Hero moments in Anacrusis, stats in Glass, philosophy in Vinyl.
- **Mobile** — Same design language, OLED option for battery. Touch replaces hover — tilt and glow disabled, press states added.
- **Print** — Business cards, packaging, merch. Pantone spot colors for bronze. Navy foundations.
- **Events & experiential** — Navy backdrops, bronze accent lighting, frosted acrylic panels, grain textures on projection surfaces.
- **Social media** — Dark palette for feed posts, Anacrusis headlines at large sizes, Satoshi for body text.
- **Email** — Dark mode with light mode fallback. Satoshi only (Anacrusis won't load in email clients).
- **Investor materials** — Full brand treatment. The manifesto is the pitch — the design system IS the proof of quality.
- **Partner-facing** — Co-branded guidelines. Our room, their music. The brand flexes to accommodate artist expression without losing its identity.


## The Test

If something feels flat, add texture.
If something feels sharp, round it.
If something feels fast, slow it down.
If something feels static, let it breathe.

The test is always the same: does this feel like a place you'd want to sit and listen to music in?
